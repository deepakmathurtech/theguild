APPENDIX E: LEGAL RISKS REQUIRING ADVOCATE REVIEW BEFORE LAUNCH
================================================================================

1. Payment Aggregator / Payment Gateway (PA/PG) Classification (RBI) —
   Depending on how Guild's payment flow is structured (e.g., whether funds
   ever touch a Guild-controlled account versus passing directly through a
   licensed PA/PG), Guild itself may be treated as a payment aggregator
   under RBI's PA/PG guidelines, triggering separate licensing
   requirements. This must be reviewed against Guild's actual technical
   payment architecture.
2. Nodal / Escrow Account Requirements — The "temporarily hold funds before
   settlement" model (Payout Policy) may require use of a nodal or escrow
   account under RBI rules; counsel should confirm the payment gateway
   partner's arrangement satisfies this.
3. GST Registration & Place of Supply — Guild's own GST treatment of the
   Platform Commission (especially for pan-India Organizations and any
   international Users/Organizations) should be reviewed with a tax
   advisor, particularly place-of-supply rules for online marketplace
   services.
4. TDS Obligations under Section 194-O / Section 194C — If Guild is
   treated as an "e-commerce operator" facilitating payments for
   Organizations, Section 194-O TDS obligations under the Income Tax Act
   may apply and should be confirmed with a tax advisor.
5. Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020 Applicability — Whether
   Guild qualifies as a "marketplace e-commerce entity" under these Rules
   (with attendant disclosure obligations) should be formally confirmed,
   since the Rules were originally drafted with goods/services e-commerce
   in mind and Guild's Quest/Event model is somewhat novel.
6. DPDP Act 2023 Rules Notification Timeline — The DPDP Act's substantive
   compliance obligations (consent manager framework, data breach
   notification timelines, cross-border transfer restrictions) depend on
   rules that may be notified in phases; this package should be revisited
   once the rules are finalized.
7. Guild Card / Guild Passport as a "Credential" — If a Guild Card or Guild
   Passport is ever presented to third parties (e.g., employers) as a
   professional credential, Guild should assess whether this creates
   representations/liability akin to a certifying body, and whether
   additional disclaimers or a standalone policy (see Appendix C, item 8)
   are needed before that feature is promoted externally.
8. Liability Cap Enforceability — The liability cap in Terms of Service
   Section 15.2 (limited to Platform Commission received or a fixed INR
   amount) should be reviewed against Indian Contract Act principles on
   unconscionable/one-sided terms, especially since Guild is dealing with
   individual consumers (Users) who may benefit from consumer-protection
   scrutiny of exclusion clauses.
9. Cross-Border Users/Organizations — If Guild permits sign-ups from
   outside India, export control language (ToS Section 16.11) is a
   placeholder only; actual applicability of foreign sanctions/export
   control regimes should be assessed if this is a real use case.
